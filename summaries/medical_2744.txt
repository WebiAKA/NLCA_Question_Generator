This passage describes a typical operating room surgical procedure. An anterior cervical discectomy and arthrodesis with 8 mm lordotic ACF spacer is administered to a patient with cervical spondylosis and herniated nucleus pulposus of C4-C5. This surgery requires the patient to be in an extended, supine position so the neurosurgeon can access the herniated nucleus pulposus of C4-C5 without causing additional strain to the neck. Next, a shoulder roll is placed between the scapula while the head is resting on a doughnut to maintain neck extension. The surgeon marks an incision at the C4-C5 level on right side of his patient's neck; after opening it with a #10 blade knife and dissecting down through subcutaneous tissues, they reach the avascular plane which they cut bluntly into until they reach anterior cervical fascia.
