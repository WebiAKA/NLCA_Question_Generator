This is a 17-year-old African-American female who was seen for abnormal uterine bleeding following spontaneous vaginal delivery without complications at the time. She has had abnormal uterine bleeding with an ultrasound showing a 6 cm x 6 cm fundal mass suspicious either for retained products or endometrial polyp, and so she underwent dilation and curettage of the uterus and hysteroscopy with IV sedation, paracervical block. The abdomen was prepped and draped in the normal sterile fashion. Her bladder was drained which produced 100 cc of clear yellow urine. A bimanual exam was performed by Dr. X and Dr. Z and the uterus found to be anteverted, mobile, fully involuted to a pre-pregnancy stage as well as the cervix and vagina being normal with no obvious masses or deformities.
