In a patient with left carpal tunnel syndrome, surgery was done by opening the upper extremity through a transverse incision one fingerbreadth proximal to the distal volar wrist crease. The antebrachial fascia was cut in a distally based fashion. A good plane was positively identified and the upper extremity was prepped and draped in the usual standard fashion. Great care was taken with placement and it continued until the release of transverse carpal ligament done under direct vision through the incision.
